Make a Blanket Sew-In

Details

Description

Blue Bar Quilts in Middleton is opening their classroom for an afternoon of sewing with other Project Linus Blanketeers. Bring your hand-sewing (binding, anyone?) or your sewing machine and current Linus project and join us for the afternoon. If you're ready for a new Linus project, or need materials for your current one, take advantage of the 15% discount that Blue Bar is offering for materials purchased for Project Linus quilts.

Please note that Project Linus will not be providing fabric, kits, or batting at this event. A Project Linus volunteer or two will be on hand that afternoon to answer questions about Project Linus, collect any completed blankets, and of course, work on their own quilt.

Registration is not mandatory, but preferred, due to classroom capacity. This activity will run during the store's normal business hours of 12 - 4 pm.

    About the agency

    Project Linus National Headquarters is located in Belton, Missouri. National President Patty Gregory and Vice President Mary Balagna direct and orchestrate the activities of Project Linus chapters located across the United States. Patty has been involved with the organization in the Kansas City area since April 2000 and Mary in Central Illinois since late 1998, as chapter coordinators and now as directors and officers. Mary also maintains a very busy chapter, donating an average of 350 blankets every month to local children. With chapters in all 50 states, Project Linus continues to grow. Blankets are collected locally and distributed to children in hospitals, shelters, social service agencies, or anywhere that a child might be in need of a big hug.
